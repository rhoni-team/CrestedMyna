Crested Myna

Photo taken in the front yard of one the authors of these demo, in Juana Koslay, San Luis, Argentina, during the winter of 2018.

The Crested Myna or Chinese Starling (Acridotheres cristatellus) is a bird mostly black black plumage, with a conspicuous frontal crest and white wing patches. It is a member of the family Sturnidae, which includes other mynas such as the Common Myna (Sturnus vulgaris).

The Crested Myna is native to China, Laos, Myanmar, Burma and Vietnam. They prefer open habitats as agricultural lands, and urban areas.

This species was introduced on many countries for several reasons. In some cases, it was introduced as a pest control, like in the case of Philippines, or as a cage bird that was later released or that could scape, like in the case of Canada. In some countries it is currently well stablish. In Argentina, for example, flocks of hundreds of birds can be found near La Plata river.

In this project we analyzed the the distribution of this species around the world, and present statistics and spatial analysis to have some insights about its expansion and colonization.
